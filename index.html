Introduction

Several models exist for the transmission of SARS-CoV-2 (severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2) based on varying assumptions and parameters. The significance of this problem is that because models vary depending on the parameters and underlying assumptions, there is uncertainty on how to filter out the valid and optimal inputs. In this replication study, we develop a simple infection rate model based on the results and parameters reported by current research.




Our Research

Our model is scaled-down to represent a single room for simplicity. The assumptions and calculations made are based on findings from the MIT Indoor Safety Guidelines and Cambridge models. We implemented the parameters given by the MIT model, specfically physical room dimensions and physiological factors (human and virus). We validated our calculations with the plots provided by the airborne.cam tool.
